Introduction

Welcome to the UNS Framework, an open-source initiative that offers a technically-driven approach to MQTT and Unified Namespace (UNS) through the use of YAML files.

i We're just getting started. Join us, and stay tuned for more updates soon!

Describe What You Have

The UNS Framework emphasises describing what you have without changing what or how you do it, ensuring seamless integration with your existing systems. Inspired by standards like Docker and Kubernetes, the UNS Framework establishes a universal standard that is both human-readable and machine-readable.

It is not a software product but a descriptive standard that seamlessly integrates with any new or existing UNS deployments. It complements existing standards like Sparkplug, OPC/UA, and MT Connect by focusing on describing what you have.

By articulating your UNS with the framework, you enable seamless integration and understanding across documentation, applications, analytics, security tools, and AI systems.

# uns.yml — describe your namespace
version: '1.0'
uns:
name: 'UNS Framework'
region: 'UK'
timezone: 'UTC'
topic_structure: 'version/site/area/...'
topic_standard: 'ISA95'
information_model: 'User Defined'
 
# site.yml — describe your factory
site:
- name: UK
id: UK
area:
- name: Area 1
line:
- name: Line 1
cell:
- name: Cell 1

Overview

The UNS Framework is designed to create a standardized method for defining UNS and MQTT using the simplicity and flexibility of YAML.

Non-Intrusive Standardisation

Similar to how Docker or Compose files do not restrict container operations, the UNS Framework is not intended to replace existing standards such as Sparkplug, OPC/UA Information Models like Umati, or initiatives like MT Connect. Instead, it complements these efforts by focusing on describing what you have without imposing changes on what or how you do it.

Compatibility and Flexibility

The UNS Framework is not a software product but a descriptive standard that seamlessly integrates with any new or existing UNS deployments. It is designed to harmonise with your current systems, enhancing interoperability without imposing changes on your existing infrastructure.

Templates for Efficiency

To simplify the integration of equipment and sensors, the UNS Framework includes templates built on the same user-friendly YAML format. These templates promote efficient integration and reuse, accelerating the deployment process.

Paradigm Shift

The UNS Framework introduces a paradigm shift in how MQTT and Unified Namespace (UNS) are defined and managed.

Traditional methods can be complex and cumbersome, especially for large-scale applications. By focusing on a descriptive standard rather than a prescriptive software product, the UNS Framework simplifies the process, making it more accessible and adaptable to a wide range of use cases.

Core Definitions

The UNS Framework configuration encompasses sites, areas, lines, cells, equipment, sensors, functions, producers, consumers, MQTT brokers, and databases — everything described in YAML.
